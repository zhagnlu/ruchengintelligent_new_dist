Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our plate rolling machines

Q:What is the difference between 3-roll and 4-roll plate rolling machines?

3-roll machines are more economical and suitable for general applications, but require pre-bending the leading edge separately and may leave longer straight edges. 4-roll machines offer higher precision and efficiency with integrated pre-bending capability, allowing edge bending without removing the plate. They produce shorter remaining straight edges and eliminate flat spots, making them ideal for high-precision work and thick plate rolling.

Q:What materials and thicknesses can be rolled?

Our plate rolling machines can process carbon steel, stainless steel, aluminum alloys, and other ductile metals. Different models are available to handle various plate thicknesses. The actual bending performance depends on material ductility, hardness, and yield strength. Softer materials and those with higher ductility are easier to roll to smaller diameters.

Q:How accurate is the bending process?

Our CNC-controlled plate rolling machines achieve high bending precision for diameter consistency. The CNC system allows precise control of roll positions and automatically calculates the correct settings based on plate thickness and desired radius. Digital feedback systems monitor the rolling process in real-time to maintain consistent quality throughout the operation.

Q:What is pre-bending and why is it important?

Pre-bending is the process of bending the leading and trailing edges of the plate before the main rolling operation. This prevents flat spots on the finished cylinder and reduces the remaining straight edge. Our 4-roll machines have integrated pre-bending capability, allowing you to pre-bend edges without removing and reinserting the plate, which saves time and improves quality.

Q:What maintenance is required?

Regular maintenance includes checking hydraulic oil levels and quality, inspecting roll surfaces for wear, lubricating bearings and moving parts, and verifying alignment of the roll system. The hydraulic system should be serviced annually with oil changes and filter replacements. With proper maintenance, our machines are designed for 20+ years of reliable service. We provide comprehensive maintenance manuals and training with each machine.

Q:Can the machine roll conical shapes?

Yes, with the optional conical rolling attachment, our machines can produce conical shapes by adjusting the roll positions to create a gradual taper. The CNC system calculates the required roll positions for the desired cone angle. This capability is particularly useful for manufacturing hoppers, funnels, and conical sections for pressure vessels. Standard cylindrical machines can be retrofitted with this option.
