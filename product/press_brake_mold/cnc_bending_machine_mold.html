Tooling Types & Features

Comprehensive range of precision-engineered press brake dies and punches for diverse bending applications

V-Dies (Bottom Dies)

The most common type of press brake tooling, V-dies feature varying die opening widths to accommodate different material thicknesses and bend angles. Available in single, double, and multi-groove configurations. Precision ground surfaces ensure accurate bend angles and consistent repeatability. Standard lengths: 500mm, 515mm, 835mm.

Punches (Upper Tools)

Precision-machined upper tooling with various nose radii and angles for different bending applications. Standard punch angles include 88¬∞, 90¬∞, and custom profiles. Heat-treated to HRC 45-58 for superior wear resistance. Compatible with quick-change segmented tooling systems for fast setup and changeover.

Gooseneck Dies (Return-Flanging)

Specialized tooling designed for forming return flanges and working with pre-bent parts. The unique profile provides clearance for existing flanges, preventing interference during secondary bending operations. Essential for complex sheet metal fabrication requiring multiple bends and tight geometries.

Arc Bending Mold (Radius Dies)

Specialized curved tooling designed to form smooth radius bends and arcs with consistent curvature. Features precision-machined radius profiles (R5, R10, R15, R20, etc.) for creating rounded corners, cylindrical sections, and decorative curves. Essential for HVAC ductwork, pipe fittings, architectural metalwork, and decorative applications. Eliminates the need for post-bending forming operations while ensuring uniform radius along the entire bend length.

Custom Profile Dies

Engineered custom tooling for specialized bending applications including U-channels, Z-profiles, radius bends, offset bends, and complex geometries. Manufactured to customer specifications with tight tolerances. Ideal for high-volume production requiring consistent, repeatable custom profiles.

Lower Die (V-Die / Bottom Die)

The foundation component of press brake tooling featuring precision-machined V-grooves in various opening widths (6V, 8V, 12V, 16V, etc.). Constructed from hardened chromium molybdenum steel with ground surfaces for superior accuracy. Available in single-groove and multi-groove configurations to accommodate different material thicknesses. The V-opening determines the minimum bend radius and is critical for achieving accurate bend angles. Features secure mounting channels for stable clamping to the press brake bed, ensuring consistent positioning and repeat accuracy across production runs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about press brake tooling and bending molds

Q:What material is best for press brake tooling?

Chromium molybdenum steel (42CrMo, Cr12MoV) is the industry standard, offering exceptional strength, wear resistance, and corrosion protection. For heavy-duty applications or extended tool life, premium HSS (high-speed steel) or carbide materials can be specified. The material choice depends on production volume, material being bent, and required tool life.

Q:How do I select the correct V-die opening?

The general rule is to use a V-die opening that is 8 times the material thickness (8T rule). For example, 2mm thick material requires a 16mm die opening. This ratio ensures proper bend formation, prevents cracking, and maintains consistent bend angles. Thicker materials or harder metals may require wider die openings.

Q:What hardness should press brake tooling have?

Standard press brake tooling typically has a hardness of HRC 45-52, which provides excellent wear resistance while maintaining toughness to prevent cracking. For demanding applications or hard materials (stainless steel, high-tensile steel), tooling with HRC 54-58 hardness is recommended. Higher hardness extends tool life but requires careful handling to avoid chipping.

Q:Can you manufacture custom profile dies?

Yes, we specialize in custom tooling for unique bending applications. Provide us with your part drawings, material specifications, and bend requirements. Our engineering team will design custom dies for U-channels, Z-profiles, radius bends, offset bends, or any special geometry. Custom tooling ensures consistent results for high-volume production runs.

Q:How long does press brake tooling last?

Tool life depends on material being bent, bending frequency, tonnage applied, and maintenance. Quality heat-treated tooling can last 100,000+ bends for mild steel. Stainless steel or high-tensile materials reduce tool life. Proper maintenance (cleaning, storage, periodic inspection) and correct die selection maximize longevity. Worn tooling can be reground to restore performance.

Q:What's the difference between European and American tooling standards?

European tooling (Wila, Amada, Trumpf systems) uses segmented quick-change tooling with standardized clamping systems for rapid setup. American-style tooling typically uses full-length dies with mechanical or hydraulic clamping. European systems offer greater flexibility and faster changeover, while American systems provide robust, economical solutions. We manufacture tooling compatible with both standards.
