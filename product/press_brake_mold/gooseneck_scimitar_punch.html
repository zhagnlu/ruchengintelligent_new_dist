Gooseneck Tooling Variations

Comprehensive range of gooseneck and offset punch configurations for diverse return flanging and box forming applications

Standard Gooseneck Punch

Traditional gooseneck design with deep relief for channel forming and return flanging. Features symmetric profile optimized for right angle bends. Available in various throat depths and face widths to accommodate different flange lengths. Hardened to HRC 47¬±2 for durability. Ideal for standard box and pan forming operations requiring interference clearance.

Gooseneck Scimitar Combination

Advanced hybrid design combining gooseneck deep relief with scimitar offset profile. Provides exceptional clearance for complex multi-bend operations where both depth and offset are required. Enables formation of deep channels with return flanges in confined geometries. Custom engineered for specialized box forming and container manufacturing applications.

Offset Saber Punch

Specialized offset punch with saber/scimitar profile for creating Z-bends and offset flanges. Forms two parallel bends in single operation with guaranteed parallelism. Ideal for overlapped assemblies and joining applications. Available in various offset distances and bend angles. Precision ground surfaces ensure consistent repeatability.

Acute Angle Gooseneck

Gooseneck punch with acute angle tip (30¬∞, 45¬∞, 60¬∞) for specialized applications requiring sharp bends with deep relief clearance. Commonly used for hemming operations, acute channels, and specialized box corners. Compatible with standard V-dies. Heat-treated for edge retention on sharp tip geometry.

Variable Relief Gooseneck

Adjustable gooseneck system allowing variable throat depth for different return flange lengths without tooling changes. Modular design accommodates multiple flange dimensions. Quick-change components reduce setup time for job shops. Provides versatility for low to medium volume production with varying flange requirements.

Custom Profile Gooseneck

Engineered custom gooseneck profiles for specialized applications requiring unique geometries. Designed to customer specifications based on part drawings and production requirements. Can incorporate radius bends, compound angles, or specialized clearance features. Ideal for high-volume production of proprietary box and channel designs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about gooseneck and scimitar press brake tooling

Q:What is the difference between a gooseneck punch and a standard punch?

A gooseneck punch features a deep relief design (offset profile) that provides clearance for existing flanges and deep box geometries. Standard straight 90¬∞ punches would cause interference when trying to form channels or parts with return flanges. Gooseneck punches enable multi-bend operations by preventing the punch from hitting previously formed flanges, making them essential for box forming, door frames, and channel bending applications.

Q:What does "scimitar" mean in press brake tooling?

"Scimitar" or "saber" refers to an offset, curved profile design similar to the curved blade of a scimitar sword. In press brake tooling, scimitar punches feature an offset geometry that allows for complex channel formations and Z-bends. When combined with gooseneck design, it creates a specialized tool providing both deep relief clearance and offset capability for the most challenging box and channel forming operations.

Q:How do I select the correct throat depth for my gooseneck punch?

Throat depth should be determined by the return flange length you need to form. Measure the distance from the bend line to the edge of your existing flange, then add clearance (typically 3-5mm). For channels formed in two strokes, the throat depth determines the maximum channel depth. Provide us with your part drawings showing flange dimensions, and our engineering team will recommend the optimal throat depth and face width for your application.

Q:Can gooseneck punches be used for hemming operations?

Yes, acute angle gooseneck punches (30¬∞, 45¬∞, 60¬∞) are specifically designed for hemming and acute bending operations where deep relief clearance is required. These combine the interference-free benefits of gooseneck design with sharp tip geometry for hemming. Two-stage hemming operations (acute bend followed by flattening) can be performed even on parts with existing flanges using specialized gooseneck hemming tooling.

Q:What materials and hardness should gooseneck punches have?

Standard gooseneck punches are manufactured from T10A, 42CrMo, or Cr12MoV alloy steel with quenching hardness of HRC 47¬±2. This hardness provides excellent wear resistance while maintaining toughness to prevent cracking in the deep relief geometry. For high-volume production or hard materials (stainless steel, high-tensile steel), optional induction hardening or Nitrex¬Æ surface treatment can increase surface hardness while maintaining core toughness.

Q:Do you manufacture custom gooseneck profiles?

Absolutely. We specialize in custom-engineered gooseneck punches designed to your exact specifications. Provide part drawings showing flange dimensions, bend angles, material specifications, and production volume. Our engineering team will design custom gooseneck tooling optimized for your specific application, including unique throat depths, compound angles, radius bends, or specialized clearance features. Custom tooling is particularly cost-effective for high-volume production of proprietary box and channel designs.
