Radius Tooling Types & Methods

Comprehensive range of arc mold tooling solutions for diverse radius bending applications and material requirements

Steel Radius Punches

Precision-machined steel punches with fixed radius profiles from R5 to R100mm. Manufactured from heat-treated 42CrMo steel with HRC 45-52 hardness for durability. Tip radius designed at minimum 63% of material thickness to prevent stress concentration. Ideal for standard production radius bending with consistent repeatability.

Urethane Radius Dies

High-strength polyurethane bottom dies providing mar-free bending for pre-finished and pre-painted materials. Forgiving urethane material achieves clean bends without surface marking or scratching. Compatible with standard V-die holders. Ideal for architectural panels, appliances, and decorative metalwork requiring pristine surface finish.

Large Radius Tooling Systems

Custom-engineered systems for forming large radius arcs (R80-R200mm) that exceed standard press brake table limits. Features modular segmented design for flexible radius adjustment. Compatible with CNC press brakes for automated radius control. Suitable for architectural metalwork, transportation equipment, and large curved panels.

Bump Forming Tooling

Specialized tooling for creating large radius arcs using multi-step bump forming technique. Approximates smooth arc with series of small incremental bends. Uses standard V-dies and punches with CNC programming for consistent results. Cost-effective solution for large radius applications without dedicated radius tooling investment.

Adjustable Radius Systems

Versatile tooling allowing multiple radius options using same bottom die by changing interchangeable radius punches. Reduces tooling inventory requirements while maintaining flexibility. Quick-change mounting for rapid setup between different radius specifications. Ideal for job shops handling diverse radius bending requirements.

Hybrid Steel-Urethane Dies

Advanced tooling combining steel core structural strength with urethane surface layer for mar-free bending. Provides durability of steel with surface protection of urethane. Suitable for high-volume production requiring both strength and surface quality. Extended tool life compared to pure urethane with maintained mar-free performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about arc mold and radius bending tooling

Q:What is the difference between bump forming and radius tooling?

Bump forming uses standard V-dies and punches with CNC programming to create large radius arcs through multiple small incremental bends (approximating the arc with linear segments). Dedicated radius tooling uses specially profiled punches and dies to create smooth radius bends in a single operation. Bump forming is cost-effective for occasional large radius work, while dedicated radius tooling provides superior surface finish and faster production for regular radius bending applications.

Q:When should I use urethane dies instead of steel?

Use urethane dies when bending pre-finished, pre-painted, or polished materials where surface marking is unacceptable. Urethane provides mar-free bending ideal for architectural panels, appliances, decorative metalwork, and any application where surface quality is critical. Steel dies are preferred for higher production volumes, harder materials, or when surface marking isn't a concern. Hybrid steel-urethane combines both benefits.

Q:How do I select the correct radius for my application?

The punch radius should be at least 63% of material thickness to prevent stress concentration. For air bending, the internal radius forms at approximately 15-20% of the V-die opening width. Match your punch tip radius to the natural internal radius created by the die opening for consistent angles. Consider material springback (stainless steel requires 1-2¬∞ compensation). Consult our technical team with your material specifications for optimal radius selection.

Q:Can you manufacture custom radius profiles?

Yes, we specialize in custom radius tooling for unique applications. Provide your required radius dimensions, material specifications, production volume, and part drawings. Our engineering team will design custom arc molds optimized for your specific application. Custom tooling is particularly cost-effective for high-volume production runs requiring consistent radius specifications not available in standard tooling catalogs.

Q:What is the maximum radius I can achieve on a press brake?

Maximum achievable radius depends on press brake table width and tooling system. Standard radius punches typically range from R5 to R100mm. Large radius tooling systems can achieve R120-R200mm. For radii exceeding these limits, bump forming can create virtually any radius through programmed multi-step bending. Large radius applications may require specialized extended-reach tooling or custom engineered solutions.

Q:How do I minimize springback in radius bending?

Springback increases with material hardness and decreases with larger bend radius. To minimize springback: use properly matched punch radius (63% minimum of material thickness), apply sufficient tonnage for bottom bending rather than air bending, compensate bend angle 1-2¬∞ for stainless steel, use larger die opening to reduce stress, and consider coining for zero springback applications. Material-specific springback tables help predict and compensate for springback in production.
