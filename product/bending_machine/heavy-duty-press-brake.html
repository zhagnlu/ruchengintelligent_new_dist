Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about heavy-duty press brakes

Q:What maximum material thickness can heavy-duty press brakes handle?

Depending on tonnage and material type, heavy-duty press brakes can bend mild steel up to 50mm thick, stainless steel up to 30mm, and aluminum up to 40mm. The exact capacity depends on the machine tonnage, working length, and material properties.

Q:How does the crowning system work?

The automatic crowning system compensates for frame deflection by adjusting the table curvature. It uses CNC-controlled hydraulic or mechanical wedges to create a slight convex curve in the table, counteracting the natural bowing that occurs under heavy loads, ensuring consistent angle across the entire bend length.

Q:Can these machines run 24/7?

Yes, heavy-duty press brakes are specifically designed for continuous industrial production. They feature oversized cooling systems, industrial-grade components, and robust construction to handle 24/7 operation. Regular maintenance schedules should be followed to ensure optimal performance.

Q:What foundation requirements are needed?

Heavy-duty press brakes require reinforced concrete foundations due to their significant weight and operational forces. Foundation specifications vary by machine size but typically require 500-800mm thick concrete pads with proper reinforcement. We provide detailed foundation drawings with each machine.

Q:What is the typical delivery time?

Heavy-duty press brakes are typically custom-engineered to specifications. Lead time ranges from 90-180 days depending on tonnage, configuration, and optional features. Rush delivery may be available for certain configurations.

Q:What warranty and support is provided?

We provide a comprehensive 3-year warranty on heavy-duty machines covering all major components. This includes on-site installation, commissioning, operator training, and 24/7 technical support. Extended warranty packages and preventive maintenance programs are available.
