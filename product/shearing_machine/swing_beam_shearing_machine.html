Components

Better understand the machine performance through detailed component descriptions and technical features

Frame Structure

The machine frame is constructed from high-quality steel with a welded structure design. The compact and simple pendulum arm mechanism provides excellent rigidity and stability, ensuring straight and accurate cuts throughout the machine's operational life.

Swing Beam Mechanism

The innovative swinging blade design creates an arc-shaped cutting motion that ensures material straightness and clean cuts. This mechanism provides fast working speed with good stability, ideal for processing thin to medium thickness materials efficiently.

Hydraulic System

Equipped with high-quality hydraulic components, the system provides stable and reliable cutting force. The hydraulic drive ensures smooth operation with low noise levels and excellent energy efficiency. Built-in oil cooling system maintains optimal operating temperature.

NC Back Gauge System

The motorized ball screw back gauge provides precise positioning with extended travel distance options. NC controller allows for programmable movements and automatic positioning, significantly improving efficiency and accuracy for repetitive cutting operations.

Blade System

High-quality tool steel blades with four usable cutting edges provide long service life and consistent cutting quality. Manual single-point blade gap adjustment allows for optimization based on material thickness, ensuring clean cuts with minimal burr formation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our hydraulic swing beam shearing machines

Q:What is the difference between swing beam and guillotine shears?

Swing beam shears use a pendulum arm design with an arc-shaped cutting motion, offering advantages like a smaller footprint, good stability, and fast working speed. They are ideal for thin to medium thickness materials. Guillotine shears use a vertical blade motion and are better suited for thicker materials requiring higher precision.

Q:What materials can be cut with this machine?

Our swing beam shearing machines can process various metal materials including mild steel, stainless steel, aluminum, copper plates, and other metal sheets. The maximum thickness depends on the material type and machine model. Please contact us for specific capacity options for your application.

Q:How often do the blades need to be replaced?

The blades are designed with four usable cutting edges, which significantly extends their service life. Blade life depends on material type, thickness, and cutting frequency. Under normal operating conditions, blades can last for tens of thousands of cuts before requiring rotation or replacement.

Q:Is training provided for operators?

Yes, we provide comprehensive operator training as part of our installation service. Our technical team will train your operators on machine operation, safety procedures, routine maintenance, and troubleshooting. Training materials and operation manuals are also provided.

Q:What is the typical delivery time?

Standard configurations typically ship within 30-45 days from order confirmation. Custom configurations may require 60-90 days depending on specifications. We'll provide a specific timeline when you place your order and keep you updated throughout the manufacturing process.

Q:What maintenance is required?

Regular maintenance is simple and straightforward: check hydraulic oil levels weekly, clean the machine daily, lubricate moving parts according to the schedule, and inspect blade condition regularly. We provide a detailed maintenance guide and schedule. Most routine maintenance can be performed by operators.
