Blade Design & Manufacturing

Understand the advanced engineering behind our high-performance crusher blades

High-Grade Alloy Steel

Our crusher blades are manufactured from premium alloy steels including D2, SKD11, HSS, 9CrSi, and T10. Each material is selected for optimal balance of hardness, wear resistance, and impact strength. High carbon steel provides excellent hardness, while alloy steel offers superior wear resistance and toughness. Material certification ensures consistent quality and performance.

Four Blade Type Options

We offer four specialized blade types: Claw type for aggressive cutting of thick materials, Flat type optimized for thin-walled products under 3mm, Flake type for general-purpose applications, and V type for precision cutting. Each design is engineered for specific material characteristics and processing requirements, ensuring optimal particle size and production efficiency.

Precision Manufacturing Process

Our manufacturing process includes cutting by sawing machine, rough grinding by milling machine for dimensional accuracy, drilling by precision drilling machine for mounting holes, vacuum heat treatment in controlled furnaces to achieve HRC 56-60 hardness, and final precision grinding to create sharp, consistent cutting edges. This multi-step process ensures superior blade quality and performance.

Rigorous Quality Control

Every crusher blade undergoes comprehensive quality inspection including dimensional verification, hardness testing (HRC 56-60 according to customer requirements), edge sharpness evaluation, and visual inspection for defects. Dynamic testing simulates actual operating conditions to verify cutting performance. Complete quality documentation and material traceability are provided with each order.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about crusher blades

Q:How long do crusher blades typically last?

Blade life depends on material type, crusher speed, and blade material. High-speed steel blades typically last 150-300 hours of continuous operation. Hard alloy (carbide-tipped) blades can reach 800-1200 hours when avoiding metal impurities. Proper maintenance, avoiding metal contaminants, and regular blade inspection maximize service life. We recommend keeping spare blade sets for minimal downtime.

Q:Which blade type should I choose for my application?

Flat blades work best for thin-walled products (under 3mm) like bottles and films, offering higher output. Claw type blades are ideal for thick materials and provide aggressive cutting action. Flake type is versatile for general-purpose applications. V type offers precision cutting for specialty materials. Our technical team can recommend the optimal blade type based on your specific material characteristics and production requirements.

Q:Can crusher blades be sharpened and reused?

Yes, most crusher blades can be professionally resharpened 2-4 times depending on wear severity and blade design. Resharpening restores cutting edges and extends total blade life. However, blades with severe chipping, cracks, or excessive wear should be replaced rather than resharpened. We offer professional blade sharpening services with quick turnaround times and quality guarantees.

Q:How do I know when blades need replacement?

Signs include increased particle size, uneven granulation, higher power consumption, excessive noise, visible blade damage or rounding, and reduced throughput. Inspect blades every 50-100 operating hours. Replace when cutting edges show significant wear or if output quality declines. Preventive replacement before complete failure minimizes downtime and maintains consistent product quality.

Q:What blade material offers the best value?

For occasional use or soft plastics, high carbon steel provides good value. For commercial operations, 9CrSi or SKD11 alloy steel offers excellent balance of performance and cost. For high-volume production or abrasive materials, consider D2 tool steel or carbide-tipped blades despite higher initial cost - their extended life often provides better total cost of ownership. We can help analyze your specific ROI based on production volume and material type.

Q:Do you provide installation and setup guidance?

Yes, comprehensive installation instructions are provided with all blade orders, including proper blade alignment, gap adjustment, and torque specifications. Our technical support team assists with setup, troubleshooting, and performance optimization. We provide safety guidelines and best practices documentation. Training materials and video tutorials are available to help your operators maximize blade performance and safety.
