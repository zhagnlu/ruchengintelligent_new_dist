Tooth Design & Engineering

Understand the precision engineering behind our custom toothed blade solutions

Custom Tooth Patterns

We offer a comprehensive range of tooth designs including V-toothed, diamond-toothed, convex-toothed, and serrated patterns. Each tooth profile is precision-engineered to create specific perforation characteristics. The tooth spacing (pitch) is customizable from 0.5mm to 50mm to achieve your desired cut-to-tie ratio and tear strength.

Precision CNC Manufacturing

Each toothed blade is manufactured using advanced CNC machining technology for exceptional accuracy. The process includes precision cutting of the blade shape, followed by CNC grinding or wire EDM to create perfectly uniform teeth with consistent geometry. Dimensional tolerances are maintained to ¬±0.01mm for optimal performance.

Premium Material Selection

Material selection is critical for toothed blade performance. HSS provides excellent balance of hardness and toughness for paper and thin plastics. Tungsten carbide offers superior wear resistance for demanding applications and abrasive materials. Food-grade stainless steel is available for applications requiring FDA compliance.

Comprehensive Quality Testing

Every toothed blade undergoes rigorous quality inspection including dimensional verification of tooth pitch, depth, and profile. Edge sharpness testing ensures consistent cutting performance. Dynamic balance testing for rotary blades guarantees smooth operation at high speeds. Complete traceability documentation provided with each order.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about toothed blades

Q:How do I select the correct tooth pitch for my application?

Tooth pitch selection depends on your desired tear strength and perforation quality. Smaller pitch (closer teeth) creates easier tear-off with lower strength. Larger pitch provides stronger material between perforations. Our engineering team can recommend optimal pitch based on material type, thickness, and end-use requirements. Sample testing is available to verify performance.

Q:What materials can toothed blades perforate?

Toothed blades can perforate paper, cardboard, plastic films, laminates, foils, textiles, and specialty materials. Material capability depends on blade material and tooth design. HSS blades handle most paper and plastic applications. Carbide blades are recommended for abrasive materials, coated substrates, or high-volume production requiring extended blade life.

Q:How long do toothed blades last?

Blade life varies significantly based on material processed, production speed, and tooth design. HSS blades typically last from hundreds of thousands to millions of linear feet in paper applications. Carbide blades can last 5-10 times longer than HSS. Proper setup, blade pressure, and regular inspection maximize blade life. We provide expected life estimates based on your specific application.

Q:Can toothed blades be re-sharpened?

Some toothed blade designs can be re-sharpened, while others are designed for replacement. Rotary toothed blades with simple tooth profiles can often be professionally re-ground. Complex tooth patterns or very fine pitches typically require replacement. Re-sharpening feasibility depends on original tooth depth, wear patterns, and dimensional requirements. We offer evaluation services to determine if re-sharpening is cost-effective.

Q:What information do I need to provide for a custom blade quote?

For accurate custom quotes, provide: blade outer diameter, inner diameter (bore size), hub thickness, desired tooth pitch, tooth profile type if known, material being processed, material thickness, production speed, and any special requirements. Technical drawings or samples of existing blades are extremely helpful. Our team can work from minimal information to develop complete specifications.

Q:Do you offer sample testing before production orders?

Yes, we offer sample blade production and testing services for custom applications. Sample blades allow you to verify perforation quality, tear strength, and production performance before committing to full production quantities. Sample fees are often credited toward production orders. This service is especially recommended for new applications or challenging materials.
