Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about shearing machine blades

Q:How long do shearing machine blades last?

Blade life depends on material being cut, thickness, frequency of use, and maintenance. With four usable edges, our blades can last 4 times longer than single-edge blades. Typical service life ranges from tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands of cuts. Proper blade gap adjustment and regular rotation extend blade life significantly.

Q:What materials can these blades cut?

Our shearing blades can cut mild steel, stainless steel, aluminum, copper, brass, and other metal alloys. The maximum cutting thickness depends on material hardness and machine capacity. Standard blades handle materials up to 50mm thick in mild steel. For harder materials like stainless steel, specialized blade materials are recommended.

Q:How do I know when to rotate or replace blades?

Signs include increased burring, rough cut edges, visible nicks or chips in the cutting edge, increased cutting force required, or material deformation. When these occur, rotate the blade to use a fresh edge. Replace when all four edges show significant wear. Regular visual inspection after every shift helps maintain optimal cutting quality.

Q:Can damaged blades be repaired or re-sharpened?

Blades with minor wear can sometimes be professionally re-ground to restore cutting performance. However, deep nicks, cracks, or severe wear typically require replacement. Re-grinding reduces blade dimensions and may affect machine blade gap settings. We offer professional blade inspection services to determine if re-grinding is cost-effective for your situation.

Q:What blade material should I choose for my application?

For general mild steel cutting, Cr12MoV or 6CrW2Si provides excellent performance and value. For stainless steel and harder materials, consider high-speed steel (HSS) or tungsten carbide. For high-volume production, premium materials offer better long-term cost-effectiveness despite higher initial cost. Our technical team can recommend optimal materials based on your specific application.

Q:Do you provide installation and setup support?

Yes, we provide comprehensive installation guidelines, blade gap adjustment procedures, and setup recommendations with every blade order. Our technical support team is available to assist with installation questions, troubleshooting, and optimization. On-site installation services are available for large orders or complex applications.
